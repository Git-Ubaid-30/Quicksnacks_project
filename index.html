Our Best Recipes

Here are some Varieties of Healthy Snacks Which are best for your Health & teste try now.

Coconute-Raspberry

This beautiful raspberry smoothie is deliciously sweet and tart at the same time, with a subtle tropical touch brought by the coconut milk. A quick & healthy smoothie.

Oatmeal

Oatmeal, a favorite food of many nutritionists, comes from an ancient cereal grain. It's an important world crop as it require as many nutrients as wheat.

Trail-mix

Trail mix is one of the most popular snacks available.Besides being flavorful, filling, and portable, it is easy to customize based on your personal preferences.

Graham-Crackers

Graham crackers simple & easy snacks you might hand to the children in your life. They are sweet and crunchy snack enjoyed by kids and adults & its a healthy choice.

chia-pudding

Chia seeds are incredibly rich in nutrients.The nutrients in chia seeds may promote heart health & support strong bones, and improve blood sugar management.

Roasted-chickpeas

Roasted Chickpeas have nutty taste. As a rich source of vitamins, minerals & fiber, chickpeas may offer a variety of health benefits & reducing your risk of disease.

Chessy-cracker

Cheese and crackers can provide a source of healthy carbohydrates and protein. Crackers made with whole grains provide a source of dietary fiber, an indigestible carbohydrate.

Peach-Mango

Peach Mango Smoothie is easy to make recipe & is made within 5 ingredients. It can be made both with fresh or frozen fruit so that you can enjoy it.

Applesauce

Applesauce contains antioxidants called phytochemicals. These antioxidants may help to reduce your risk of cancer, diabetes, and heart disease.

Cottage-cheese

Cottage cheese makes for a wonderful snack. Sometimes referred to as curds and whey, this dairy product differs from cheese & it is sold and served fresh.

Saltine-crackers

Saltine crackers are noted for their dry texture & distinctive perforations that make them easy to snap & enjoy.They are often paired with soup, nut butter & jelly.

Spinach

The leafy greens like spinach in green smoothies are a great source of vitamin K1. Research has shown that people with intake of vitamin K1 have a lower risk of bone fractures.
